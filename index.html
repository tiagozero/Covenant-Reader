⚡ Words Mining
Capture words as you read. Send them to VocabForge to study later.
How Word Mining Works
1 · ADD

Type a word into the Quick Capture form and press + Mine. Pronunciation and definition are optional but useful for VocabForge study later.

You can also send words here from a future reader integration (text-selection mining).

2 · STORE

Words are saved in your browser's local storage under the key pera-mv-mined.

Cap: 500 words. Auto-purge: entries older than 7 days are removed on each load. Duplicates are rejected.

3 · STUDY

Click Send to VocabForge to load your mined words into the SRS session. From there they go through Quiz, Flashcard, or Multiple Choice — and start building a level.

Or use Download CSV to export and re-import as a permanent vocabulary deck via the Upload tab.

⚠️ Mining is a temporary scratchpad — not permanent storage. Export to CSV to keep words long-term. ✗ Mined words are not automatically added to your CSV decks. You control when they enter the SRS.

CSV Word Priority & Colors
How priority works: When the same word appears in more than one CSV, the first CSV uploaded always wins — it controls the highlight color and the tooltip data shown for that word. Later CSVs are ignored for any word already claimed by an earlier one. The upload order matches the order the chips appear in the Core tab.

Longer matches take precedence over shorter ones regardless of CSV order — e.g. if CSV 2 has "New York" and CSV 1 only has "New", the phrase "New York" will be highlighted using CSV 2's color.

Backup & Restore
What's saved: All CSV vocabulary sets, VocabForge study progress, Words Mining list, ignored words, and your reader settings.

How it works: Click Download Backup to save a .json file to your device. To restore, click Restore Backup and select that file — your current data will be replaced. Backups are stored locally; nothing is sent to a server.
